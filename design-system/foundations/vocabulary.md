# Vocabulary

Australian English throughout. The locked audience nouns are **practitioners** and **clients** — never "coaches" as the blanket term.

## Words to prefer
**About us:** operating system, structure, automation, calm, behind the scenes, handled, in one place, command.
**About the user:** your practice, your clients, your day, your craft, practitioner.
**About outcomes:** runs itself, fewer no-shows, time back, nothing slips, follow-up that sends itself, activated clients.

## v2 product-texture words
The command-palette structure invites a small set of "OS" words — use them where they're true, not as decoration:
**command, palette, run a command, type `/`, search, workspace, surface, one place, replaces.** The thin ticker and mono labels carry this texture; the copy should match it (concise, lowercase mono labels, plain commands).

## Vertical-specific vocabulary
Health OS serves wellness practitioners across a few shapes:

| Vertical | People | Ops | Moments |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coaching practices | clients, members | programs, check-ins | session, milestone |
| Therapists / practitioners | clients | intake, notes, scheduling | appointment, follow-up |
| Studios | members, attendees | classes, memberships | booking, drop-in |
| Wellness businesses | clients, leads | pipeline, onboarding | enquiry, activation |

> Note: a *coaching practice* is one vertical; its owner is still a **practitioner** and its people are **clients**. Keep the locked nouns even inside the coaching row.

## Generic verbs we use
run, handle, structure, automate, activate, send, remind, follow up, untangle, simplify, replace.

## Generic verbs we avoid
leverage, supercharge, unlock, revolutionise, crush, dominate, hack, 10x, empower (overused), disrupt.

## Numbers we like
Concrete and modest-but-real: "12 hrs saved a week," "98% of bookings kept," "3× faster onboarding," "0 dropped follow-ups." These pair naturally with the `CountUp` stat component. Avoid invented precision and vanity metrics.

## "Replaces" framing (tool cards)
A tool card names the disconnected tool Health OS replaces. Keep it factual and Australian-plain: "Replaces your booking app," "Replaces the reminders spreadsheet." Name the job, not a competitor by brand unless there's a real reason. No gloating — the Sage states the consolidation calmly.

## Headline recipe
`[Their mess] + [what now happens by itself]` — e.g. "Bookings, reminders and follow-up — running while you're with clients." Plain verb, specific noun, no adjective stack, sentence case.
