# Voice

Grounded, confident, intelligent — never salesy. A calm **Sage** advisor who has already untangled the mess and is quietly showing you how it works. Short, clear sentences. Quietly powerful. **Australian English.** Outcome first, no hype.

## 8 voice rules
1. **Outcome first.** Lead with what changes for the practitioner, then the mechanism. "No-shows drop because reminders send themselves" beats "boost your business."
2. **Specific over generic.** Name the real thing that happens, not an adjective about it.
3. **Second person.** Talk to *you* and *your practice*, not "users" or "businesses."
4. **Short sentences.** One idea each. Cut the qualifier.
5. **Name their world.** Bookings, intake, follow-up, **clients**, **practitioners**, practice — their nouns. Never "coaches" as the catch-all, never "leads" / "funnels" unless it genuinely fits.
6. **No hype, no AI jargon.** No "leverage," "synergy," "revolutionary," "supercharge," "unlock," "10x." No exclamation marks in product copy. No urgency theatre.
7. **Promise specifics, not adjectives.** Show the mechanism behind the result.
8. **Australian English** throughout — spelling and idiom (see house style below).

## Audience language — locked
Say **"practitioners"** and **"clients"**. Never call the audience "coaches" as a blanket term — coaching is one shape of practice among many. Practitioners run practices; practices serve clients.

## 3-second feeling test
A practitioner should feel: *"This already understands my mess — and it's calm about it."* Relief, not adrenaline.

## Tone by context
| Context | Register |
|---|---|
| Homepage hero | Confident, plain, specific — the one true thing |
| Command-bar / search hero | Inviting and concrete — "type / for commands," not clever |
| Feature page | Show the mechanism; calm and concrete |
| Tool-card / comparison | Matter-of-fact — name the tool it replaces, no smugness |
| Pricing | Direct, unfussy, no urgency |
| Case study | Quiet proof; let the numbers and the practitioner talk |
| Onboarding | Reassuring, one step at a time |
| Empty state | Encouraging, points to the next small action |
| Email (marketing) | A one-to-one note from someone who gets it |
| Email (transactional) | Plain, factual, fast |
| Error | Own it, fix it, move on — never cute |
| 404 | Light, brief, redirect |
| About | Grounded story, no founder mythology |
| Social | Useful first; one idea per post |

## Grammar & house style (Australian English)
- **Sentence case** everywhere — headlines, buttons, nav, labels. (Overlines / tracked mono labels may be uppercase.)
- **Australian spelling:** organise, optimise, colour, behaviour, centre, enrolment, licence (noun), specialise, programme→**program** (AU software/biz convention). When in doubt, follow the Macquarie Dictionary.
- Oxford comma on.
- Numerals for numbers (12 hrs, 3×, 98%).
- No exclamation marks in product / marketing body copy.
- Contractions are welcome — they sound human.
- One space after full stops.
- Dates: 11 June 2026 (day-month-year).

## When there's nothing specific to say
Say less. A short, plain sentence beats a padded one. If a claim isn't true or specific, cut it — don't dress it up. The Sage doesn't fill silence with noise.
